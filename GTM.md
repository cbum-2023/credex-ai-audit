# GTM

The exact target user is the technical founder or first engineering manager at a seed to Series A startup with 5-40 employees, a small finance function, and fast-growing AI tool usage. They are not looking for a procurement platform yet; they are looking at a Cursor, Claude, OpenAI, or ChatGPT invoice and wondering whether the team is being disciplined or simply normalizing waste.

Right before they want this tool, they Google things like “Cursor business worth it,” “Claude team vs pro,” “OpenAI API bill high,” or scroll X posts about AI coding tools getting expensive. They also see teammates casually adding seats to tools without a budget owner.

The best zero-budget channels are founder and engineering communities where spend anxiety is already visible: Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA cost threads, Hacker News “Show HN,” Latent Space Discord, AI Engineer Discord, and seed-founder Slack groups. I would also reply directly to public posts complaining about AI bills with a specific audit screenshot, not a generic product link.

First 100 users in 30 days: publish a teardown of five public AI stacks, launch a Show HN post, DM 50 founders with a personalized “I found one likely waste pattern in your stack” note, ask three user interviewees for two intros each, and turn every high-savings audit into an anonymized chart for X and LinkedIn. The unfair Credex channel is discounted credit inventory: Credex can show real savings without forcing teams to rip out tools.

Week-1 traction if it works: 150 completed audits, 25 email captures, 8 high-savings leads above $500/month, 4 booked consultations, and at least 10 users sharing public report URLs.
