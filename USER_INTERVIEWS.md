# User Interviews

This document captures insights from short user interviews conducted during product validation. The goal of these conversations was to understand how teams currently manage SaaS spending, identify operational inefficiencies, and validate whether the audit workflow provides meaningful value.

## Interview 1

- Name / Initials: Neha Gupta
- Role: Co-founder & CTO
- Company Stage: Seed-stage SaaS Startup
- Key Quotes: "We adopted AI tools very quickly, but we never created a proper process to review recurring subscriptions."
- Most Surprising Insight: Teams were more concerned about visibility into spending than reducing costs aggressively.
- Impact on Product / Design Decisions: Added clearer monthly spend summaries and categorized tools by usage type.

---

## Interview 2

- Name / Initials: Arjun Mehta
- Role: Product Manager
- Company Stage: Growth-stage B2B Company
- Key Quotes: "A simple recommendation with estimated savings is far more useful than a complex analytics dashboard."
- Most Surprising Insight: Users preferred concise summaries and actionable suggestions over visual-heavy reports.
- Impact on Product / Design Decisions: Simplified the report interface and removed unnecessary charts and visualizations.

---

## Interview 3

- Name / Initials: Sarah Chen
- Role: Operations Lead
- Company Stage: AI Automation Agency
- Key Quotes: "Different departments buy their own AI tools, so no one has a complete picture of overall spending."
- Most Surprising Insight: Users trusted deterministic financial calculations more than AI-generated recommendations.
- Impact on Product / Design Decisions: Kept the audit engine deterministic and positioned AI summaries as optional supporting insights.
