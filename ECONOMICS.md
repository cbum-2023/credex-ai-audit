# Economics

Assume a converted Credex customer buys $30,000/year in discounted AI credits. If Credex earns a 12% gross margin on the spread, gross profit is $3,600/year. If retention is two years, rough LTV is $7,200 before support cost. A high-savings audit lead is valuable because the product has already quantified pain and urgency.

Channel CAC can stay low early. Founder community posts cost time only; at 10 hours/week and an assumed $50/hour opportunity cost, 25 audits from that work implies $20 per audit. Targeted founder DMs might take 4 hours for 50 messages; if 8 complete audits, CAC per audit is about $25. Partner posts with AI tooling newsletters could be performance-based; a $300 sponsorship that drives 60 audits is $5 per audit.

A viable funnel might be: 100 audit completions -> 20 email captures -> 6 consultations booked -> 2 credit purchases. At $7,200 LTV each, that cohort is worth $14,400. Even if time-adjusted CAC is $2,000, the payback is attractive. The product becomes unprofitable if booked-call conversion falls below 1% or if most “savings” are under $100/month, because sales time would exceed expected margin.

For this to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex would need roughly $8.3M in annual credit GMV at 12% margin. At $30k/customer/year, that is about 278 active customers. If 10% of completed audits become qualified high-savings leads and 20% of those buy, the tool needs about 13,900 completed audits over 18 months, or roughly 770 per month. That requires repeatable distribution beyond launch spikes.
