# Metrics

The North Star metric is qualified savings discovered per week: total monthly savings from completed audits where the user submits an email and savings exceed $500/month. This fits the product better than DAU because buyers do not need to use an AI spend audit every day; the business outcome is finding serious, reachable savings.

Three input metrics drive it. First, audit completion rate from landing page visitor to results page, because the product must deliver value quickly. Second, email capture rate after the result, because Credex cannot follow up without permission. Third, high-savings consultation rate, because only serious savings justify human sales time.

I would instrument page view, first tool added, audit submitted, result rendered, public link copied, email captured, and consultation clicked. I would also store anonymized tool mix and savings band so pricing-rule gaps become obvious.

A pivot trigger: after 1,000 completed audits, if fewer than 3% produce qualified savings above $500/month or fewer than 10% of qualified users book a consultation, the offer is not sharp enough. The pivot would be toward narrower audits, such as “AI coding tool spend for engineering teams,” instead of a broad AI stack audit.
